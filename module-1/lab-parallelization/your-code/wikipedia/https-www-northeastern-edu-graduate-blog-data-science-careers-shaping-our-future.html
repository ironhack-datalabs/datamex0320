11 Data Science Careers Shaping Our Future

\r\n
\r\n
\r\n By Ashley Eneriz\r\n  | \r\n November 23, 2018\r\n
\r\n

For three years in a row, data scientist has been named the number one job in the U.S. by Glassdoor. What’s more, the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics reports that the rise of data science needs will create 11.5 million job openings by 2026. Not only is there a huge demand, but there is also a noticeable shortage of qualified data scientists.

\n

Daniel Gutierrez, managing editor of\xc2\xa0insideBIGDATA, told Forbes, \xe2\x80\x9cThe word on the street is there\xe2\x80\x99s definitely a shortage of people who can do data science.\xe2\x80\x9d If you have a passion for computers, math, and discovering answers through data analysis, then earning an advanced\xc2\xa0degree in data science might be your next step.

\n

What is Data Science?

\n

Dr. Martin Schedlbauer, data science professor at Northeastern University, says that data science is used by \xe2\x80\x9ccomputing professionals who have the skills for collecting, shaping, storing, managing, and analyzing data [as an] important resource for organizations to allow for data-driven decision making.\xe2\x80\x9d Almost every interaction with technology includes data\xe2\x80\x94your Amazon purchases, Facebook feed, Netflix recommendations, and even the facial recognition required to sign in to your phone.

\n

Amazon is a prime example of just how helpful data collection can be for the average shopper. Amazon\xe2\x80\x99s data sets remember what you’ve purchased, what you’ve paid, and what you’ve searched. This allows Amazon to customize its subsequent homepage views to fit your needs. For example, if you search camping gear, baby items, and groceries, Amazon will not spam you with ads or product recommendations for geriatric vitamins. Instead, you are going to see items that may actually benefit you, such as a compact camping high chair for infants.

\n

Similarly, data science can be useful for reminding you of habitual purchases. If you order diapers every month, for example, you might see a strategically placed coupon or deal around the same time each month. This use of data is meant to act as a trigger, prompting you to think, \xe2\x80\x9cI just remembered I need to buy diapers, and I should buy them now because they are on sale.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

Data science benefits both companies and consumers alike. McKinsey Global Institute found that big data can increase a retailer\xe2\x80\x99s profit margin by 60 percent, and \xe2\x80\x9cservices enabled by personal-location data can allow consumers to capture $600 billion in economic surplus,\xe2\x80\x9d meaning they are able to purchase a good or service for less than they were expecting. For example, if you budgeted $7,500 to purchase a jacuzzi and then found the exact model you wanted for $6,000, your economic surplus would be $1,500. Data science can simultaneously increase retailer profitability and save consumers money, which is a win-win for a healthy economy.

Data Science Is Helping the Future

\n

Data science enables retailers to influence our purchasing habits, but the importance of gathering data extends much further.

\n

Data science can improve public health through wearable trackers that motivate individuals to adopt healthier habits and can alert people to potentially critical health issues. Data can also improve diagnostic accuracy, accelerate finding cures for specific diseases, or even stop the spread of a virus. When the Ebola virus outbreak hit West Africa in 2014, scientists were able to track the spread of the disease and predict the areas most vulnerable to the illness. This data helped health officials get in front of the outbreak and prevent it from becoming a worldwide epidemic.

\n

Data science has critical applications across most industries. For example, data is used by farmers for efficient food growth and delivery, by food suppliers to cut down on food waste, and by nonprofit organizations to boost fundraising efforts and predict funding needs.

\n

In a 2015 speech, Economist and Freakonomics author Steven Levitt said\xc2\xa0that CEOs know they are missing out on the importance of Big Data, but they do not have the right teams in place to perform the skills. He says, \xe2\x80\x9cI really do believe still that the combination of collaborations with firms\xe2\x80\x99 big data and randomization [\xe2\x80\xa6] is absolutely going to be at the center of what economics is and what other social sciences are going forward.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

Pursuing a career in data science is a smart move, not just because it is trendy and pays well, but because data very well may be the pivot point on which the entire economy turns.

\n

In-Demand Data Science Careers

\n

Data science experts are needed in virtually every job sector\xe2\x80\x94not just in technology. In fact, the five biggest tech companies\xe2\x80\x94Google, Amazon, Apple, Microsoft, and Facebook\xe2\x80\x94only employ one half of one percent of U.S. employees. However\xe2\x80\x94in order to break into these high-paying, in-demand roles\xe2\x80\x94an advanced education is generally required.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cData scientists are highly educated\xe2\x80\x9388 percent have at least a master\xe2\x80\x99s degree and 46 percent have PhDs\xe2\x80\x93and while there are notable exceptions, a very strong educational background is usually required to develop the depth of knowledge necessary to be a data scientist,\xe2\x80\x9d reports KDnuggets, a leading site on Big Data.

\n

Here are some of the leading data science careers you can break into with an advanced degree.

\n

Business Intelligence (BI) Developer

\n

Average Salary: $89,333

\n

Typical Job Requirements: BI developers design and develop strategies to\xc2\xa0assist business users in quickly finding the information they need to make better business decisions. Extremely data-savvy, they use BI tools or develop custom BI analytic applications to facilitate the end-users’ understanding of their systems

\n

Notable Companies: DollarShave Club, Discover, and Liberty Mutual

\n

Data Architect

\n

Average Salary: $137,630

\n

Typical Job Requirements: Ensure data solutions are built for performance and design analytics applications for multiple platforms.

\n

Notable Companies: IBM, eBay, AAA Club Alliance, T-Mobile

\n

Applications Architect

\n

Average Salary: $134,520

\n

Typical Job Requirements: Track the behavior of applications used within a business and how they interact with each other and with users.

\n

Notable Companies: UPS, Humana, Dow Jones, Oracle

\n

Infrastructure Architect

\n

Average Salary: $126,353

\n

Typical Job Requirements: Oversee that all business systems are working at optimally and can support the development of new technologies and system requirements. A similar job title is Cloud Infrastructure Architect, which oversees a company\xe2\x80\x99s cloud computing strategy.

\n

Notable Companies: Abbott Labs, Hewlett-Packard, Dell, Ford Motor Company

\n

Enterprise Architect

\n

Average Salary: $161,272

\n

Typical Job Requirements: According to Techopedia, an enterprise architect, \xe2\x80\x9cWorks closely with stakeholders, including management and subject matter experts (SME), to develop a view of an organization’s strategy, information, processes and IT assets.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

Notable Companies: Cisco, Boeing, Lockheed Martin, Microsoft

\n

Data Scientist

\n

Average Salary: $139,840

\n

Typical Job Requirements: Find, clean, and organize data for companies. Data scientists will need to be able to analyze large amounts of complex raw and processed information to find patterns that will benefit an organization and help drive strategic business decisions. Compared to data analysts, data scientists are much more technical.

\n

Notable Companies: Facebook, Capital One, Airbnb, Twitter

\n

Data Analyst

\n

Average Salary: $83,878

\n

Typical Job Requirements: Transform and manipulate large data sets to suit the desired analysis for companies. For many companies, this role can also include tracking web analytics and analyzing A/B testing.

\n

Notable Companies: Walmart, Gap, Bank of America, Kohler

\n

Data Engineer

\n

Average Salary: $151,307

\n

Typical Job Requirements: Perform batch processing or real-time processing on gathered and stored data. Make data readable for data scientists.

\n

Notable Companies: Spotify, Verizon, General Motors, Shutterfly

\n

Machine Learning Scientist

\n

Average Salary: $139,840

\n

Typical Job Requirements: Research new data approaches and algorithms.

\n

Notable Companies: Apple, The Johns Hopkins Hospital, Expedia, Tinder

\n

Machine Learning Engineer

\n

Average Salary: $114,826

\n

Typical Job Requirements: Create data funnels and deliver software solutions.

\n

Notable Companies: Nike, Dropbox, LinkedIn, Uber

\n

Statistician

\n

Average Salary: $93,589

\n

Typical Job Requirements: Interpret, analyze, and report statistical information, such as formulas and data for business purposes.

\n

Notable Companies: U.S. Census Bureau, Google, PayPal, U.S. Department of Agriculture

\n

Data Scientists Are in Constant Demand

\n

Dr. Schedlbauer concludes that while some data science work will likely be automated within the next 10 years, \xe2\x80\x9cthere is a clear need for professionals who understand a business need, can devise a data-oriented solution, and then implement that solution.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

Data science experts are needed in almost every field, from government security to dating apps. Millions of businesses and government departments rely on big data to succeed and better serve their customers. Data science careers are in high demand and this trend will not be slowing down any time soon, if ever.
